About Me

I'm Chandler Hutchens, an Aerospace Engineer, with a passion for space systems! I enjoy working on complex problems, and being a part of a team to solve them. I've worked on a variety of projects that have honed my skills in systems architecture, verification and validation, and mechanical/electrical design.

Throughout my time at Arizona State University, I worked on four cubesat missions, each in a varying role from technician to head systems engineer. Getting hands on experience with flight hardware early in my career has been key in my overall understanding of space systems.

In my free time, I love watching movies, playing board games, and hiking! I enjoy traveling and meeting people around the world, offering new perspective and motivations in my professional endeavors.

Thermal Vacuum Chamber

The thermal vacuum chamber (TVAC) was one of my first projects at the Interplanetary Initative. My job was to turn a high-altitude balloon chamber into a high-vacuum chamber with thermal control. By the time I graduated ASU, the chamber was capable of the following:

  • Temperature Range: -120C to 100C
  • Vacuum Range: 760 Torr to 7E-4 Torr
  • 3x D-sub 50 Feedthrough
  • 2x D-sub 25 Feedthrough
  • 1x Coaxial Feedthrough

University Projects

RS-25 Propellant System Analysis

This was the final project for MAE565 Rocket Propulsion with Professor Werner Dahm. The project was to perform an analysis of the RS-25 propulsion system and calculate parameters such as Mach number, pressure, temperature, and flow speed in each of the various pumps. After arriving at the nozzle, thrust, coefficient of thrust, and specific impulse could be determined. The full report can be downloaded below, with the calculation spreadsheet.

Report Spreadsheet

Modeling of a Mixing Chamber with the Navier-Stokes Equations

This was the final project for MAE561 Computational Fluid Dynamics with Professor Marcus Herrmann. The project was to take a mixing chamber, with given inital and boundary conditions, and model it using the continuity and Navier-Stokes equtions. I solved for the u-velocity (left), v-velocity (middle), and mass fraction (right), each resulting in a ten second video shown below. The full report can be downloaded below which includes the inital conditions, derivations, results, and the MATLAB code.

Report

Comparing Turbulence Models for Two-Dimensional Flow Over a Backward-Facing Step

This was the final project for MAE575 Turbulence with Professor Werner Dahm. This project compared three turbulence models in ANSYS Fluent agaist eachother and expeirmental data from Driver and Seegmiller (1995) for a backward-facing step. These models were compared with a coarse, medium, and fine mesh, against parameters such as Reynolds stress. The full report can be downloaded below, which includes the initial conditions and ANSYS set up, equations, results, and the MATLAB code.

Report

Comparison of Vortex Identification Methods of NACA0012

This was the final project for MAE598 Analysis and Modeling of Fluid Flows with Professor Jeonglae Kim. I obtained wind tunnel data of a NACA0012 airfoil from the experiment of Chow, J.S., Zilliac, G.G., and Bradshaw, P (1997), and used this data to compare five vortex identification methods at three positions along the airfoil. The full report can be downloaded below, which includes the initial conditions, equations, results, and the MATLAB code.

Report

Ram/Scramjet Propulsion System Analysis

This was the final project for MAE563 Aircraft Propulsion with Professor Werner Dahm. The project was to construct a MATLAB program to calculate the thermodynamic cycle of the ram/scramjet for a list of inputs. Upon completing the cycle, various parameters were solved for to better understand the engine performance for analysis or design.
